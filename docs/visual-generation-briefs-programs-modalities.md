# Shreevan Wellness Visual Generation Briefs

This file is a production-level visual brief for Google Flow image/video generation for these pages:

- `/programs/14-day-transformation`
- `/programs/28-day-inner-awakening`
- `/programs/60-day-rishi-residency`
- `/modalities/yoga-therapy`
- `/modalities/guided-meditation`
- `/modalities/sound-healing`
- `/modalities/panchkarma-detox`
- `/modalities/chakra-opening`
- `/modalities/spiritual-sadhanas`

## Global Art Direction

Use one consistent visual world across every asset:

- Premium Rishikesh wellness retreat beside Maa Ganga.
- Realistic editorial photography, not stock-photo smiles.
- Quiet luxury, simple Indian architecture, wood, stone, cotton, clay cups, neutral linen, soft green shawls.
- International guests, mostly age 35 to 60, professional/founder/seeker energy.
- Indian facilitator or host should feel grounded, calm, and credible.
- Avoid medical clinic visuals, dramatic miracle-healing visuals, neon chakra graphics, fantasy effects, visible fake text, unreadable signage, crowded tourist scenes, orange/brown heavy palettes, overly beige flat rooms, or staged influencer poses.
- Keep wellness claims visually responsible: show practice, reflection, comfort, support, and structure. Do not imply cure, treatment, trauma release, disease detox, or guaranteed transformation.

## Common Negative Prompt

Use this negative prompt style for all images:

No hospital, no doctor coat, no medical treatment, no miracle healing, no crying breakdown, no exaggerated spiritual glow, no fantasy aura, no neon chakras, no crowded tourist street, no luxury resort pool, no fake unreadable wall text, no brand logo unless specifically requested, no distorted hands, no extra fingers, no deformed faces, no plastic skin, no over-smiling stock photo, no yoga contortion, no western spa stereotype, no clinical detox equipment, no religious appropriation spectacle, no cluttered room, no dark moody depression look.

## Common Camera And Quality Direction

- Realistic editorial photography.
- Natural morning or late-afternoon light.
- 35mm or 50mm lens for human scenes.
- 24mm to 35mm lens for rooms, courtyards, and landscapes.
- Soft depth of field, but keep the main subject clearly readable.
- High dynamic range, true skin tones, detailed natural textures.
- Keep important subject in the center 60 percent of the frame for mobile cropping.
